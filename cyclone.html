 Cyclones
Dataset contains IBTrACS master dataset with the addition of ocean heat flux, precipitation, seaflux, infrared sounder retrievals, and sea surface temperatures.
✸ International Best Track Archive for Climate Stewardship (IBTrACS) --> Serves as the master dataset to which all environmental variables are joined. IBTrACS provides the position, intensity, and metadata for every recorded tropical cyclone globally, with each row representing a single storm observation at a point in time. All secondary datasets are spatially and temporally matched to each storm's recorded position and date.

✸ Ocean Heat Flux (OHF) --> Ship- and buoy-based in situ oceanographic observations. Coverage is irregular in both space and time, as measurements depend on the historical presence of vessels and instruments in a given area.

✸ Precipitation Estimation from Remotely Sensed Information using Artificial Neural Networks (PERSIANN) --> Satellite-derived precipitation. It provides near-global daily rainfall estimates on a regular latitude-longitude grid, produced by combining infrared brightness temperature observations from geostationary satellites with a neural network calibrated against rain gauge and radar data.

✸ Near-Surface Meteorology (SeaFlux) --> Satellite-derived climatological product providing near-surface atmospheric variables over the global ocean. It combines retrievals from multiple passive microwave radiometers to produce gridded daily estimates of air temperature, specific humidity, and wind speed on a regular spatial grid.

✸ Infrared Sounder Retrievals (IRS) --> Contains atmospheric profile retrievals from polar-orbiting satellite infrared sounders, including instruments such as HIRS (High Resolution Infrared Radiation Sounder). IRS observations follow discrete satellite swath tracks — narrow strips of coverage along each orbital pass. This means that on any given day, only a narrow band of the globe is observed.

✸ Sea Surface Temperature (SST) --> Satellite-derived, gap-filled daily gridded sea surface temperature analysis. It blends retrievals from multiple satellite sensors (primarily AVHRR infrared radiometers) with in situ observations to produce a spatially complete regular grid.
